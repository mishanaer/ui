# Universal Interface Redpolicy

## Purpose

Interface text helps a person complete a task. It does not decorate the screen, compensate for a broken flow, or sell at any cost.

Write text only when it does at least one useful job:

- explains what is happening;
- helps choose or complete an action;
- warns about a risk, limit, cost, term, or irreversible result;
- explains an error and recovery path;
- confirms a result;
- reduces uncertainty at the exact moment it appears.

If the interface is understandable without copy, remove the copy.

## Meaning First

Every screen must pass the novice test: if a person sees it outside the full product context, they should understand what is happening and what to do next.

Before writing, answer:

- Who is the user here?
- What do they already know?
- What are they trying to do?
- What state is the product in?
- What happens after the main action?
- Is there money, time, personal data, risk, subscription, or legal consent?
- Can this phrase be misunderstood?

## Put Important Things First

Put the main fact, action, or consequence at the start: in the heading, first line, or first sentence.

Bad: `Если не продлить подписку, доступ закончится завтра`

Better: `Продлите подписку до завтра`

## Be Concrete

Replace abstract labels with actual objects and events.

Bad: `Начисления`

Better: `Счета, налоги и штрафы`

Bad: `Внимание! По счетам установлены ограничения`

Better: `Счета арестованы`

## Remove Ambiguity

Write so the sentence cannot be understood another way. This matters most for money, commissions, limits, subscriptions, dates, addresses, personal data, and destructive actions.

Bad: `Комиссия получена`

Better: `Сумма уже с комиссией`

## Do Not Lie

Do not promise a result the product cannot guarantee. Distinguish search from finding, sending from delivery, usual timing from guaranteed timing.

Bad: `Мы найдем счета по адресу`

Better: `Поищем счета по адресу`

Bad: `Деньги придут сразу`

Better: `Обычно деньги приходят сразу, но иногда перевод занимает до 3 дней`

## Explain Strange Things

If the flow contains something unexpected, explain what it is and why it is there. Use extra words when they prevent confusion or support tickets.

Good explanations answer:

- Why are we asking for this?
- Why did this appear now?
- What does this unfamiliar term mean?
- What happens if the user ignores this?

## Keep Sequence

Describe actions in the order the user performs them.

Bad: `Создайте шаблон, выбрав операцию`

Better: `Выберите операцию и создайте шаблон`

## Stay Consistent

Use one name for one object, action, and state across the product. Interface copy is not an article: do not replace repeated terms with synonyms for beauty.

Prefer repeated clarity over varied confusion:

- `Добавить в корзину` should not become `Отправить в корзину` for the same action.
- `Платеж` and `операция` should not swap if they mean the same product object.

## Risk And Legal Sensitivity

For money, legal consent, privacy, identity, taxes, credit, blocking, deletion, cancellation, or subscription, write explicitly:

- amount and currency;
- commission and whether it is included;
- source of funds or data;
- exact deadline or typical timing;
- who receives data or money;
- what happens after the button;
- whether the action can be undone.
